Victor Frankl

Holocaust survivor, founder of Logotherapy.

Timeline of Victor Frankl's life:

  • 1905 - born on March 26th at Vienna
  • 1914-1918 - experienced the hardship of 1st World War
  • 1918-1923 - attends public Applied Psychology lectures and gets acquainted with Sigmund Freud
  • 1921 - 1st public lecture about "The Meaning of Life"
  • 1924 - studies medicine at the University of Vienna Medical School
  • 1926 - presents public lecture about "Logotherapy"
  • 1931-1932 - starts his medical career at Maria Theresien Schloessl
  • 1937 - opens private practice as doctor of neurology and psychiatry
  • 1940 - becomes director of Neurological Department of the Rothschild Hospital
  • 1941-1942 - marries Tilly Grosser whom he met as a director
  • 1942 - 2nd World War begins, arrested together with his wife by the nazi
  • 1944 - selected to be transferred to a labor camp
  • 1945 - camp liberated by U.S. troops, and later finds about the death of his wife
  • 1946 - becomes director of Vienna Neurological Policlinic, and later started writing the book "Man's Search For Meaning"
  • 1947 - marries Eleonore Schwindt and gives birth to their daughter Gabriele
  • 1955 - promoted Professor at the University of Vienna
  • 1959 - the book "Man's Search For Meaning" was published in the U.S.
  • 1966 - the book "The Will To Meaning" was published
  • 1997 - published his last book "Man's Search For Ultimate Meaning" and later dies of heart failure on September 2nd
source: https://www.viktorfrankl.org/biography.html
